EXO: We Are One

EXO is a South Korean-Chinese boy group formed by SM Entertainment in 2012, known for their powerful vocals, sharp choreography, and genre-blending music that spans K-pop, R&B, and EDM. Originally debuting with twelve members divided into subunits for Korean and Chinese markets, EXO quickly gained international fame with hits like Growl and Monster, earning numerous awards for their musical achievements and stage performances.

Beyond music, the group is recognized for their dynamic concepts, often incorporating fantasy and sci-fi themes, as well as their strong fanbase called EXO-L, which has supported them through various lineup changes and solo projects. They are celebrated not only for their talent and visuals but also for influencing the global K-pop scene.

Skills

EXO is a multi-talented group known for their vocals, rapping, and synchronized dancing.

Roles

EXO is a South Korean-Chinese group under SM Entertainment.
Known for its nine active members. The group is recognized for
its unique, supernatural superpowers concept.

Leader

A K-pop leader is the designated member responsible for guiding the group,
acting as the primary liaison between the members and the agency,
and representing the group in public, interviews, and award ceremonies.

Lead Vocalist

A Lead Vocalist in a K-pop group is the member generally recognized as
the second-best singer after the Main Vocalist.
They typically possess high technical skill, strong stability,
and a distinct vocal color that allows them to lead the vocal flow of a song.

Main Dancer

A main dancer in K-pop is the member with the highest technical skill,
proficiency, and often the best performance ability in the group.
They are responsible for leading dance practices, performing dance breaks,
and often anchoring the choreography during complex, center-focused moments.

Main Vocalist

A Main Vocalist in K-pop is generally the most technically skilled singer in a group,
responsible for carrying the most challenging vocal parts.
They are key to the group's sound, often handling high notes,
ad-libs, and the majority of lines during the chorus.

Main Rapper

A Main Rapper in K-pop is the member with the highest rapping proficiency
and best technique in a group, generally responsible for the most complex, fastest,
or longest rap verses. They are often the primary lyricist for their parts and,
in some cases, write or produce the rap sections entirely.

Visual

In K-pop, the Visual is an official or unofficial position assigned
to the member(s) deemed the best-looking, specifically according to
strict Korean beauty standards. This role is a key marketing strategy
designed to attract public attention and drive popularity.

Maknae

In K-pop, the maknae is the youngest member of a group.
Derived from Korean, it refers to the last-born member, who is
often portrayed as the innocent, "baby" of the group.

Group Performance

EXO, debuting under SM Entertainment in April 2012, rapidly established
themselves as a powerhouse in the K-pop industry, often dubbed "Kings of K-pop" and
"Nation's Pick". Their performance since debut is characterized by massive commercial
success, including becoming the first artist to sell over 1 million copies of an album
in 12 years with their 2013 breakout hit XOXO and Growl.
